# 📘 Day 15: Evaluation Basics – Important Q&A

---

## ❓ 1. What is Evaluation in RAG?

Evaluation measures how well the RAG system retrieves relevant information and generates accurate answers.

---

## ❓ 2. Why is Evaluation important in RAG?

Evaluation helps detect:

* poor retrieval
* hallucination
* irrelevant answers
* weak grounding

---

## ❓ 3. What is Retrieval Evaluation?

Retrieval evaluation checks whether the retrieved chunks are relevant to the user query.

---

## ❓ 4. What is Generation Evaluation?

Generation evaluation checks the quality of the final answer generated by the LLM.

---

## ❓ 5. What is Precision?

Precision measures how many retrieved chunks are actually relevant.

Formula:

Precision = \frac{Relevant\ Retrieved}{Total\ Retrieved}

---

## ❓ 6. What is Recall?

Recall measures how many relevant chunks were successfully retrieved.

Formula:

Recall = \frac{Relevant\ Retrieved}{Total\ Relevant}

---

## ❓ 7. What is Hallucination?

Hallucination occurs when the LLM generates incorrect or fabricated information not supported by the retrieved context.

---

## ❓ 8. What is Faithfulness?

Faithfulness checks whether the generated answer is supported by the retrieved context.

---

## ❓ 9. What is Groundedness?

Groundedness means the answer is based on retrieved information rather than imagination.

---

## ❓ 10. Why does Retrieval Quality matter?

Poor retrieval leads to irrelevant or inaccurate answers because the LLM depends on retrieved context.

---

## ❓ 11. What is LLM-as-a-Judge?

LLM-as-a-Judge is an evaluation approach where one LLM evaluates another LLM’s answer quality and faithfulness.

---

## 🎯 Summary

* Evaluation checks retrieval and answer quality
* Precision and recall measure retrieval performance
* Faithfulness and groundedness measure answer quality
* Better retrieval improves final responses
